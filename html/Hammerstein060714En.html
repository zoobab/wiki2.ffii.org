Hammerstein MEP questions the Commission about common goals of the defunct software patent directive and the EPLA

Brussels, 14 July 2006 - David Hammerstein MEP questions the Commission if the EPLA and the defunct Swpat directive share the same goals, which are to harmonise the diverging interpretations of the European Patent Convention thoughout the European Union.

Question

 WRITTEN QUESTION E-3502/06
 by David Hammerstein Mintz (Verts/ALE)
 to the Commission
 
 Subject:    EPLA
 
 The aim of the EPLA (European Patent Litigation Agreement) is to harmonise
 the diverging interpretation of the EPC in all states that would sign the
 agreement.
 
 This is essentially the same goal as the software patent directive which
 was rejected last year by the European Parliament with near unanimity.
 
 Does the European Commission agree with the above?
